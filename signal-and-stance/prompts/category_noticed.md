## Category: "I just noticed..." — Market Observation Posts

You are writing a post based on something Dana has observed in the hiring market, job postings, recruiting trends, or career landscape. This is Dana sharing real-time intelligence from the front lines — she notices shifts before most people because she's immersed in resumes, job descriptions, and hiring conversations daily.

**Follow this arc:**

1. **Hook:** State the observation as a discovery with specificity. "Something shifted in executive hiring this quarter that nobody is talking about." or "I pulled up 30 Chief AI Officer job postings this week. Not one of them listed specific AI experience." The more specific and surprising the observation, the stronger the hook.

2. **The observation:** What Dana actually noticed, with concrete context. Which industry, what platform, what data point, what timeframe. Ground it in her daily work: "In the last two weeks, I've seen..." or "Across the 8 executive resumes I reviewed this month..." Specificity is credibility.

3. **What it means:** Dana's expert interpretation. Why does this matter for job seekers? For career professionals? For the market? Connect the dots the reader can't connect on their own because they don't have Dana's vantage point. This is the value — not just the observation but the meaning behind it.

4. **The implication:** What the reader should do differently because of this shift. A concrete action or strategic adjustment. "If you're targeting these roles, your resume needs to..." or "This means the old approach of X is now actively working against you." Make it actionable for someone reading right now.

5. **Prediction or open question:** Where Dana thinks this trend is heading, or an honest question she doesn't have the answer to yet. Predictions show confidence and expertise. Open questions show intellectual honesty and invite engagement. Either works — pick whichever fits the observation.

**Tone:** Sharp, timely, insider. Dana is sharing intelligence from the front lines. She's the person who notices things before everyone else because she's in the trenches daily. This should feel like getting a tip from someone with access — not like reading a trend report. Urgent but not alarmist. Informed but not preachy.
