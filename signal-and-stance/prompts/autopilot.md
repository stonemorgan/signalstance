## Step 1: Search for Content

Search the web for recent news (last 7 days) related to ANY of these topics:

- Executive hiring trends, leadership transitions, C-suite movements
- Resume and career strategy advice (especially anything data-driven)
- ATS technology, recruiting technology, AI in hiring
- LinkedIn platform changes or algorithm updates
- Salary and compensation data, especially at the executive level
- Major layoffs, hiring surges, or workforce restructuring at notable companies
- Remote work policies, return-to-office mandates, workplace flexibility shifts
- Career transitions, career pivots, especially at the senior/executive level
- Federal hiring, government resume requirements
- Board governance, board seat appointments

## Step 2: Evaluate What You Found

**Prioritize:**
- Data-driven stories (BLS reports, surveys, compensation studies, research papers) over opinion pieces
- Stories that Dana's specific audience (VPs, directors, C-suite, board members) would care about
- Stories with a specific, concrete data point or finding Dana can anchor a post around
- Stories that challenge conventional wisdom or reveal a surprising trend

**Skip:**
- Stories that are too generic ("the job market is tough right now")
- Stories about a specific small company's internal hiring unless there's a broader lesson
- Stories that are purely about entry-level or early-career topics
- Anything Dana couldn't credibly comment on as a resume/career strategist

## Step 3: Quality Gate

If you cannot find anything genuinely relevant and interesting — something that would make Dana think "I need to post about this" — then respond with exactly:

Do NOT force a weak topic. Dana's brand depends on every post being worth reading.

## Step 4: Synthesize an Insight

Once you've selected a topic, determine which of Dana's 4 content categories fits best:

- If it's a recurring trend or pattern → frame as "I keep seeing..." (pattern)
- If it addresses a common misconception or FAQ → frame as "A client asked me..." (faq)
- If it's breaking news or a fresh observation → frame as "I just noticed..." (noticed)
- If Dana would disagree with the prevailing narrative → frame as "Hot take" (hottake)

Generate the insight observation first — this is what Dana would have thought if she'd seen this news herself. Make it sound like her natural internal monologue, not a summary of an article.

- Stand alone — a reader shouldn't need to have seen the source material
- Sound like Dana wrote it after reading the news over coffee, not like a content mill reacting to headlines
